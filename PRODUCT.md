# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two primary user groups. **Buscadores**: people in Mexico looking for a plant nursery near them, searching by state/municipio or "cerca de mí", wanting fast results with WhatsApp/phone/directions on hand. **Dueños de vivero**: nursery business owners who register and manage their own listing (photos, horarios, especialidades), track métricas (vistas, clics WhatsApp, clics cómo llegar), and optionally pay for a **destacado** placement. Many owners are not digital-native — copy and flows should assume low tech comfort, not startup-founder fluency.

## Product Purpose

BuscaViveros is a national directory connecting plant buyers with local nurseries across Mexico, launching in Morelos, CDMX, Edomex y Puebla. Businesses register free and own their listing outright — unlike third-party curated directories (vdeviveros.com, viveroscerca.com.mx). Success looks like: viveros pre-cargados reclamando su ficha, búsquedas convirtiendo a clics de WhatsApp, and a growing base of destacados per municipio.

## Positioning

The nursery owns its listing. Every other directory in this space is curated by someone else; here the business controls its own ficha, photos, and info.

## Brand Personality

Vibrante, accesible, natural. Trustworthy enough for a small business owner to hand over their contact info and photos, warm enough for a plant buyer to feel this is made for them, never a cold enterprise tool and never childish.

## Anti-references

Not a generic, uncurated old-style directory (Yellow-Pages listing tables, no visual care). Not a cold corporate SaaS dashboard (generic blue-gray, identical cards, B2B-without-soul feel). Also carried forward from the original design spec: not Poppins-and-bright-green playful/childish — explicitly discarded early because the directory needs business credibility, not a kids'-app feel.

## Design Principles

The free tier never degrades — full listing, photos, WhatsApp, map, and search stay free forever; monetization never gates core value, since there is no free network to sell destacado into otherwise.

Destacado stays transparent — the badge is always visible, capped at 3 per municipio, so scarcity is honest rather than hidden.

WhatsApp is the primary channel, not forms — every conversion path (contact a nursery, request destacado, get notified on a solicitud) should default to WhatsApp over email or in-app messaging.

Built national, launched local — architecture and routing assume all of Mexico from day one even though the pilot only covers four states.

Credibility for the owner, warmth for the searcher — the same interface has to read as legitimate to a small business owner deciding whether to trust it with their listing, and as inviting to a consumer just looking for a nearby vivero.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA (4.5:1 text contrast, keyboard navigable, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected). Extra weight on low-digital-literacy business owners: larger touch targets (≥44px, reinforced beyond the AA minimum), plain-language copy over jargon, and forgiving flows (autosave on the registration wizard, inline validation with clear fixes) since a dueño abandoning mid-registration is a lost business.
